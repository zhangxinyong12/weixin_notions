# WeChat Original Writing

## Core positioning

Write for daydayago as:

> 35 岁前端程序员，正在用 AI 把自己从“写页面的人”，重新训练成“能做产品、能接单、能独立开发的人”。

All articles should serve this mother theme unless the user explicitly overrides it:

> 35 岁前端程序员如何借 AI 重新获得主动权。

## Workflow

1. Clarify the article goal if unclear: topic, reader, platform, desired tone, and whether web research is needed.
2. If research is requested or useful, search 3–5 credible sources. Extract only facts, angles, cases, data, disagreements, and terminology.
3. Build an original angle before drafting. Do not follow a source article’s title, paragraph order, metaphor chain, or conclusion path.
4. Fuse research with daydayago’s personal lens: frontend work, AI learning, mid-career anxiety, product thinking, full-stack practice, client work, plugins, mini programs, or independent development.
5. Draft in a human WeChat style: concrete scene first, short paragraphs, visible judgment, lived experience, practical takeaway.
6. Run originality and AI-tone checks before finalizing.

## Research rules

Use search material as raw ingredients, not as a skeleton.

Do:
- Compare multiple sources and synthesize patterns.
- Convert public information into personal judgment and practical implications.
- Add concrete scenarios: “我作为前端会怎么用”, “这对接单有什么影响”, “普通程序员卡在哪里”.
- Mention sources only when useful, especially for data, reports, or claims that need grounding.

Do not:
- Copy titles, section order, examples, jokes, analogies, or sentence rhythm from one article.
- Do synonym-replacement rewriting.
- Translate or summarize a single article into a new article.
- Fabricate personal experience, numbers, or case results.
- Present generic AI advice without a programmer-specific scenario.

## Originality transformation

Before drafting, create these four items internally or show them if the user asks:

- Source consensus: what most sources agree on.
- Source conflict: what sources disagree about or under-explain.
- daydayago angle: what a 35-year-old frontend developer learning AI uniquely sees.
- reader payoff: what the reader can do after reading.

A draft is publishable only if it adds at least two of:

- Personal experience or current experiment.
- Technical/product judgment from frontend background.
- Concrete workflow, checklist, or decision rule.
- Counterintuitive opinion.
- Realistic failure, limitation, or cost.

## WeChat writing style

Prefer:
- Opening with a real scene, conflict, or question.
- Paragraphs of 1–3 short sentences.
- Clear section headings, but not report-like headings.
- Plain Chinese, mild emotion, and specific nouns.
- Practical conclusions, not motivational slogans.

Avoid obvious AI prose:
- “在当今快速发展的时代”
- “总的来说”
- “以下是几点建议” repeated mechanically
- Excessive parallel bullet lists
- Over-balanced phrasing with no stance
- Empty words like 赋能、闭环、抓手、底层逻辑 unless the user’s tone requires them

## Content lanes

Prioritize these lanes:

1. AI 转型：前端如何学 AI、用 Codex、做 AI 应用。
2. 实战项目：Chrome 插件、小程序、MVP、自动化工具。
3. 接单变现：抖音接单、报价、需求沟通、交付复盘。
4. 产品/全栈：从页面开发到产品开发者。
5. 程序员日常：中年焦虑、家庭、学习节奏、生活复盘。

Lifestyle pieces are allowed, but tie them back to programmer growth, AI transition, agency, or mid-career choices.

## Output formats

For a new article, default to:

1. 选题判断：值得写/不值得写，以及原因。
2. 原创角度：一句话说明这篇和网上同类内容有什么不同。
3. 标题备选：5–8 个，含克制版和流量版。
4. 文章提纲：按微信阅读节奏组织。
5. 正文草稿：可直接发布但保留用户个人细节待补。
6. 风险检查：AI腔、抄袭风险、事实风险、定位偏离。

For rewriting an existing draft, default to:

1. 保留什么。
2. 删掉什么。
3. 加强什么。
4. 改写后的版本。
5. 发布前需要用户补充的真实细节。

## Final checks

Before final output, verify:

- Does it fit “35 岁前端借 AI 重新获得主动权”?
- Is there a personal or technical lens beyond public information?
- Would the article still stand if all searched sources disappeared?
- Are any sentences too close to source wording?
- Does it sound like a real programmer writing, not a content farm?
